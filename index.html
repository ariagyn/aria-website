15+ Years of Gynecologic Surgery Excellence

Dr. Sima Bina, MD, FACOG, is a Board-Certified Obstetrician & Gynecologist with over 15 years of experience and thousands of minimally invasive gynecologic surgeries performed throughout Northern Virginia. She is MIGS-focused, MSCP certified in menopause care, and Intuitive Robotic Surgery certified. Dr. Bina is recognized for her expertise in preventive gynecology, infertility, fibroids, contraception, endometriosis, PCOS, advanced medical & surgical treatment of abnormal uterine bleeding, peri-menopause & menopause care, hysteroscopy, minimally invasive laparoscopy, and robotic gynecologic surgery.

I was born in Shiraz, Iran — a place where poetry, love, and service were woven into everyday life. Raised by two physician parents and shaped by the values of good thoughts, good words, and good deeds, I learned early that caring for others is both a responsibility and a privilege.

At fifteen, I came to the United States alone, carrying little more than determination and hope. Growing up far from home taught me resilience, empathy, and the strength that women so often must find within themselves. These experiences continue to guide the way I care for my patients.

Over the past decade, I've had the honor of serving women in communities big and small — delivering thousands of babies, supporting women through their most vulnerable moments, and refining my skills as an advanced gynecologic surgeon. Every story I've been part of has deepened my belief in the common thread of womanhood: courage, complexity, and an innate drive to thrive.

In 2025, I chose to step away from large healthcare systems to build something more personal, more compassionate, and more aligned with the heart of why I became a doctor. Aria Gynecology & Surgery is the space where I can offer expert, evidence-based care while honoring the human connection at the center of medicine.

I am committed to providing surgical excellence, clear guidance, and truly individualized care — treating every woman with the dignity, attention, and compassion she deserves.

Outside of medicine, my life is grounded by my loving husband and our three children. Together, we seek out nature, adventure, and the beauty of simple moments — reminders of what matters most.

Recent Women's Health News & Updates

January 2026

1. Cervical Pre-Cancer Rates Plunge ~80% Among Young Women — Thanks to HPV Vaccination

A recent CDC report showed that, among U.S. women aged 20–24 who were screened between 2008 and 2022, rates of cervical precancer (CIN2+ and CIN3+) dropped by 79–80%.

Why this matters: This dramatic decline underscores how effective the HPV vaccine is in preventing cervical precancer — reinforcing the importance of vaccination and regular screening.

Learn more: "Trends in Cervical Precancers Identified Through Screening — United States, 2008–2022" (CDC)

2. Nearly All Cervical Cancers Are Caused by HPV — Prevention Is Real

According to the CDC, more than 9 of every 10 cases of cervical cancer in the U.S. are caused by high-risk strains of Human Papillomavirus (HPV).

Why this matters: HPV vaccination remains the cornerstone of cervical-cancer prevention. At Aria Gynecology & Surgery, we strongly recommend vaccination for eligible patients — and continue screening, even in vaccinated individuals.

Learn more: "Cancers Caused by HPV" (CDC)

3. CDC Launches Refreshed Gynecologic Cancer Awareness Campaign

The CDC recently refreshed its outreach under the Inside Knowledge campaign, highlighting the five main gynecologic cancers — cervical, uterine, ovarian, vaginal, and vulvar — and urging women to recognize warning signs and get timely care.

Why this matters: Many women know about cervical cancer — but less about other gynecologic cancers. Awareness saves lives. We support this campaign and encourage all our patients to learn their bodies, know what's normal, and come in promptly for evaluation if something changes.

Learn more: "About the Inside Knowledge Campaign" (CDC)

4. HPV Vaccine + Screening = Powerful Cancer Prevention Strategy

New research — including a 2025 Cochrane review — confirms that HPV vaccination remains highly effective in preventing cervical cancer and precancerous changes, especially when administered before exposure to the virus.

Why this matters: At Aria Gynecology & Surgery, we combine evidence-based HPV vaccination with our routine Pap/HPV screening and well-woman care. This dual strategy gives patients the strongest protection possible against cervical cancer.

Learn more: "New research confirms HPV vaccination prevents cervical cancer" (Cochrane)

5. Gynecologic Cancer Prevention Must Stay a Priority

A 2025 proposal from the U.S. federal government threatens to cut funding for the CDC's Division of Cancer Prevention and Control — potentially eliminating key education and screening programs for gynecologic cancers.

Why this matters: As an independent women's health practice, Aria Gynecology & Surgery reaffirms its commitment to offering comprehensive screening, preventive care, and patient education — regardless of shifting public funding.

Learn more: "Stop Cuts to Education, Screening & Surveillance Programs" (OCRA Hope alert)

Expanded Menopause Care at Aria Gynecology & Surgery

January 2026

Menopause is a biologically complex—and yet inevitable—stage in every woman's life. In many ways, it powerfully shapes womanhood, identity, and long-term health. Yet for far too long, the healthcare needs of women during the perimenopausal and menopausal years have received less attention than they deserve within our medical system.

As a physician, I have always felt a deep passion to better understand this transition and to continually sharpen my skills to more fully serve my patients during the pre-menopause and menopause stages of life. I am proud to share that I have recently strengthened this commitment by becoming a Menopause Society Certified Practitioner (MSCP) through The Menopause Society.

In alignment with The Menopause Society's mission—to empower healthcare professionals to improve the health of women during the menopause transition and beyond—I look forward to using these advanced resources, renewed focus, and up-to-date medical guidance to better support, educate, and empower you during this important phase of life.

Advanced Surgical Care with Faster Recovery and Better Outcomes

Minimally Invasive Gynecologic Surgery (MIGS) allows complex gynecologic conditions to be treated through tiny incisions using advanced laparoscopic and robotic technology. Compared to traditional open surgery, MIGS offers less pain, faster healing, lower infection risk, and quicker return to normal life.

According to the American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists (ACOG) and the National Institutes of Health (NIH), minimally invasive approaches are now considered the preferred standard of care for many gynecologic surgeries whenever medically appropriate.

For many women, MIGS means smaller scars, shorter hospital stays, less blood loss, lower complication rates, and faster return to work and family life.

Laparoscopic Surgery

What it is: A surgical technique using pencil-sized instruments, a small camera (laparoscope), and tiny incisions in the abdomen. This allows surgeons to diagnose and treat pelvic conditions with precision.

Why it matters: According to ACOG and SAGES (Society of American Gastrointestinal and Endoscopic Surgeons), laparoscopic surgery is associated with significantly less post-operative pain, and patients experience shorter hospital stays and quicker recovery than with open surgery. Common conditions treated laparoscopically include endometriosis, ovarian cysts, fibroids, adhesions, and ectopic pregnancy.

When open surgery is avoided: Avoiding large incisions reduces risks of infection, hernias, and long-term scar pain.

Robotic-Assisted Surgery

What it is: Robotic surgery uses computer-enhanced instruments that allow greater precision, improved depth perception, tremor-free movements, and enhanced access to complex anatomy.

Why it matters: According to ACOG and NIH, robotic surgery allows surgeons to perform complex procedures with the same small incisions as laparoscopy. It has expanded minimally invasive access for very large fibroids, severe endometriosis, dense scar tissue, and complex hysterectomies.

When neglected: Without minimally invasive options, many women are still offered unnecessary open surgery, which leads to longer recovery and higher complication rates.
